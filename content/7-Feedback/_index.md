> The following section presents my personal feedback after participating in the **Workforce Bootcamp – First Cloud AI Journey** program. These comments are based on my learning, hands-on practice, and project implementation experience throughout the program.

### Overall Evaluation

**1. Learning and Working Environment**  
The program created a serious yet open learning environment that encouraged participants to conduct independent research and communicate whenever they encountered difficulties. The content was organized into stages, progressing from fundamental knowledge to hands-on practice and project deployment. This structure helped me track my progress and gradually develop a cloud-oriented working mindset. I especially appreciated being able to work directly with AWS instead of learning only through theory.

**2. Support from Mentors and Team Admins**  
The mentors and team admins provided active support throughout the program by offering direction, learning materials, and feedback whenever participants faced problems. Their comments on architecture diagrams, AWS service selection, workshop organization, and troubleshooting helped me evaluate my project from a more practical perspective. I particularly valued the mentors' approach of asking guiding questions and encouraging participants to investigate the root cause before receiving a solution.

**3. Relevance to My Academic Major**  
The program content was highly relevant to Information Technology and supported career paths in backend development, cloud computing, and DevOps. Knowledge related to networking, security, databases, containers, monitoring, and application deployment could be directly connected to the programming fundamentals I learned at university. The High Concurrency Payment Gateway project helped me better understand how a backend application is designed, deployed, and operated in a practical environment.

**4. Learning and Skill Development Opportunities**  
The program allowed me to develop both technical and professional skills. From a technical perspective, I practiced using multiple AWS services, Java Spring Boot, PostgreSQL, Redis, Docker, and techniques for handling high-load systems. In terms of professional skills, I improved my ability to learn independently, search for documentation, write reports, present progress, receive feedback, and manage project time.

**5. Knowledge-Sharing Culture and Supportive Spirit**  
The program encouraged knowledge sharing and mutual support. Participants could discuss problems, learn from one another's experience, and observe different approaches to solving technical issues. This environment increased my motivation to learn and helped me understand that collaboration and information sharing are just as important as technical knowledge in the technology industry.

**6. Learning Materials, Resources, and Hands-On Content**  
The materials and exercises covered many important AWS service groups and provided participants with a broad foundation. Combining worklogs, blogs, workshops, and a final project helped me organize and consolidate my knowledge. However, advanced topics such as load testing, real-world troubleshooting, cost optimization, and deployment automation could be expanded with additional examples or dedicated practice sessions.

---

### Additional Questions

**What was the most satisfying part of the program?**  
The most satisfying aspect was the opportunity to practice directly and apply knowledge to a specific project. Instead of studying each AWS service separately, I connected VPC, RDS, ECR, ECS Fargate, Load Balancer, API Gateway, Redis, and CloudWatch into a complete architecture. The process of troubleshooting and adjusting the architecture also provided valuable practical experience.

**What could be improved for future participants?**  
The program could include more small-group architecture reviews, real-world troubleshooting sessions, and guidance on interpreting load-test results. A clearer milestone plan for the final project, together with evaluation criteria for each stage, would also help participants manage their time proactively and avoid concentrating too much work near the end of the program.

**Would I recommend the program to friends?**  
I would recommend the program to friends who are interested in AWS, cloud computing, backend development, or DevOps. It is suitable for participants who already have basic information technology knowledge and want a practice-oriented learning experience. However, participants need to be proactive, study independently, and allocate regular time to achieve good results.

---

### Suggestions and Expectations

* Add advanced hands-on sessions on CI/CD, Infrastructure as Code, cloud security, and cost optimization.
* Organize more project reviews, architecture analysis sessions, and experience-sharing discussions based on real production incidents.
* Provide project evaluation criteria, deployment checklists, and AWS resource cleanup checklists at the beginning of the program.
* Build a shared knowledge base of common issues so participants can search for information and contribute solutions.
* Include more short presentations or regular demonstrations to help participants improve technical communication skills.

### Future Development Expectations

I would like to continue participating in advanced activities related to AWS, DevOps, and system architecture. Given the opportunity, I would like to work on additional projects that are closer to enterprise requirements, receive architecture reviews, and obtain in-depth feedback from mentors.

### Appreciation

I sincerely thank the mentors, team admins, and members of the First Cloud AI Journey program for their support, knowledge sharing, and encouragement throughout my learning process. The knowledge and experience gained from this program provide an important foundation for my continued development in cloud computing, backend engineering, and DevOps.
