# Summary Report “FCJ Community Day - Stop Procrastinating, Start Building”

### Event Overview

The program combined AWS self-learning, Hackathon experience, serverless architecture for startups, DevOps foundations, confidence, and procrastination. The common message across the sessions was practical action: begin with a small step, build a real product, and improve continuously through experience.

### Featured Sessions

#### 1. Learning AWS through hands-on practice

**Hoang Thai Linh** introduced a learning approach based on **AWS Cloud Quest** and the open-source **Floci** tool. Gamification organizes learning into the Learn – Plan – Practice – DIY flow and makes cloud concepts easier to approach than theory alone. The session also emphasized resource and cost management to prevent unexpected AWS charges.

#### 2. Building products at LotusHacks 2026

**Nguyen Tran Minh Quan, An Khuong Huynh, and Quoc Anh Mai** shared their experience of developing products within the strict time limits of a Hackathon. The two featured projects were:

- **SynthHunter**: an AI voice verification and deepfake detection system;
- **Vortex**: a solution that supports candidate screening, assessment, and recruitment optimization.

The team explained how they defined an MVP, divided responsibilities, used AWS services, and handled technical incidents while working under significant time pressure.

#### 3. Building confidence and overcoming procrastination

**Nguyen Thi Quynh Nhu** explained that confidence is not the same as being perfect. It develops through repeated action, experimentation, and the willingness to make mistakes. **Pham Khac Uy** used the iceberg model to show that procrastination often comes from fear of failure, fear of judgment, or the belief that one's skills are not sufficient.

Practical techniques included:

- The 5-second rule to act before excuses appear;
- The 5-minute rule to commit to a very small first step;
- Dividing large tasks into short, achievable actions;
- Measuring progress by action rather than waiting to feel ready.

#### 4. Modernizing a startup architecture

**Tran Nghia** presented the development journey of **Tu Vi Dai Viet** and its migration from a traditional server model to managed AWS services. Components such as **AWS Amplify, Amazon ECS, Amazon RDS for PostgreSQL**, and **Amazon ElastiCache** were used to improve scalability, performance, and operational efficiency.

The AI system was organized into multiple layers: a knowledge layer prepared data, a processing layer assembled context and invoked models, while validation and caching improved output control and reduced token cost.

#### 5. DevOps begins with people and process

**Tran Minh Quan** emphasized that DevOps is more than Docker, Kubernetes, CI/CD, or Terraform. A sustainable operating model requires three connected foundations:

- **People**: communication, responsibility, and trust;
- **Process**: clear and repeatable workflows;
- **Technology**: tools that support automation.

Systems thinking helps teams identify root causes instead of repeatedly fixing surface-level symptoms such as missed deadlines, deployment failures, or unstable services.

### Knowledge and Value Gained

- Learned new ways to study AWS through practical quests and Digital Badges.
- Understood how to limit scope and create an MVP under time pressure.
- Recognized the connection between action, confidence, and overcoming procrastination.
- Gained additional insight into serverless architecture, managed services, and GenAI pipelines with validation controls.
- Understood that DevOps combines people, process, and technology rather than being only a collection of tools.

### Application to Study and Projects

- Use AWS Cloud Quest and hands-on labs to strengthen practical knowledge.
- Apply MVP thinking when introducing new project features.
- Break work into short milestones to reduce procrastination and track progress.
- Prefer managed services when reducing operational effort is important.
- Improve teamwork through clear ownership, communication, and regular review.

### Event Reflection

The event connected technical knowledge with self-management skills. The Hackathon and startup stories demonstrated that products are not created by waiting for ideal conditions, but through early action, rapid experimentation, and continuous correction. The strongest message was **Stop Procrastinating, Start Building**: action is the first step toward both competence and confidence.
